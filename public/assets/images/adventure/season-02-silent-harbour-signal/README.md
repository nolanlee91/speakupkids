# Adventure assets — Season 02: The Silent Harbour Signal

This directory is the narrative, visual, audio, and integration contract for
Season 02 of the standalone Adventure module.

Season 02 continues after `season-01-lost-maple-compass`, but it must remain
playable as an independent season. Adventure must not import Learn lessons or
Practice game question banks.

Claude Code owns the React/Next.js implementation. This README is the source of
truth for the Season 02 story, progression, content rules, and future assets.

## Season identity

- `seasonId`: `season-02-silent-harbour-signal`
- Display title: `The Silent Harbour Signal`
- Audience: ages 10–12
- Language target: A2+ to light B1
- Chapter count: 8
- Primary setting: Vancouver Harbour, Pine Island, and nearby coastal areas
- Core mystery: locate and recover a missing underwater research buoy before
  its battery and recorded data are lost
- Main learning modes: listening, comparing testimony, reading simple data,
  giving clear instructions, inference, and retelling

## Connection to Season 01

At the end of Season 01, Maple finds a sealed letter in the Hidden Garden.
Season 02 begins when she opens it.

The letter contains:

- a brass token from a marine research station;
- three handwritten times;
- a small audio recorder;
- the message: `Some messages are not written. They are heard.`

This connection is a narrative reward for returning players. A new player may
start Season 02 with a short recap explaining where the letter came from.
Season 02 progression must not depend on completion flags from Season 01.

## Story premise

An underwater research buoy named Blue 7 has stopped transmitting after a
storm. Its final recording contains a repeating signal mixed with engine noise,
seabirds, and an underwater call.

The buoy holds valuable recordings of an orca group moving through the region.
If Maple and her friends cannot locate it before the next large tide, its
battery may fail and the data may be lost.

This is a community science and rescue story, not a treasure hunt. There is no
villain. The mystery is solved by building and testing hypotheses.

## Narrative principles

1. Season 02 must not repeat the “collect three pieces” structure of Season 01.
2. Each chapter produces one piece of evidence, but the evidence is information
   used in reasoning rather than fragments of a magic object.
3. Listening is central, but every essential audio clue needs an accessible
   transcript or equivalent visual representation.
4. The children do not enter dangerous water or perform a rescue alone.
   Qualified research staff handle boats and physical recovery.
5. The orca recording is treated respectfully as wildlife research, not as a
   magical message spoken directly to Maple.
6. Questions must be supported by visible scene details, supplied text, or audio
   played during the chapter.
7. Each chapter ends with a clear conclusion and a reason to visit the next
   location.

## Chapter story briefs

### Chapter 01 — The Letter That Hummed

Maple opens the sealed letter from the Hidden Garden. The enclosed recorder
plays a short-short-long pattern mixed with an engine, seabirds, and a distant
underwater call. The brass token identifies the next destination as a marine
research station.

Suggested steps:

1. Short recap of the Hidden Garden and the sealed letter.
2. Inspect the token, note with three times, and recorder.
3. Listen to the mixed recording.
4. Distinguish sounds that are above water from the underwater call.
5. Put the three recorded times in chronological order.
6. Infer that the research station can identify the signal.

Language focus:

- `I can hear...`
- `It sounds like...`
- `before / after / while`
- separating observation from a guess

Reward: `Research Station Token`

Next chapter: `season-02-chapter-02`

### Chapter 02 — The Missing Buoy

At the marine research station, Dr. Maya Chen explains that Blue 7 disappeared
after a storm. A current map, maintenance log, three recordings, and photos of
similar buoys are spread across the worktable. Maple discovers that Blue 7 sent
one final signal after the storm ended, proving that it continued to move.

Suggested steps:

1. Meet Dr. Chen and learn what Blue 7 records.
2. Compare three buoy photographs.
3. Read the maintenance log and storm timeline.
4. Listen to or inspect the timestamps of three recordings.
5. Find the detail that rules out a simple battery failure.
6. Conclude that the buoy drifted away while still transmitting.

Language focus:

- `It was last checked at...`
- past simple and time expressions
- `This one is different because...`
- `It might have...`

Reward: `Blue 7 Record`

Next chapter: `season-02-chapter-03`

### Chapter 03 — Voices at the Market

A fish seller, a kayaker, and a young bird photographer each saw a blue object
drifting through the harbour. Their accounts disagree about the time and
direction. One witness has a photograph whose sun position and background
landmarks make it more reliable.

Suggested steps:

1. Interview the three witnesses.
2. Match each statement to its speaker.
3. Compare their claimed times and directions.
4. Inspect the photograph for objective clues.
5. Separate observation from opinion.
6. Choose the most reliable account and explain why.

Language focus:

- `She said that...`
- `They both saw...`
- `however`
- `I trust this clue because...`

Reward: `Harbour Photograph`

Next chapter: `season-02-chapter-04`

### Chapter 04 — The Library of Tides

The photograph shows Blue 7 near an old tide marker. At the library, Maple
finds a historical current map and a simplified tide table. The group uses the
time of the photograph and the direction of the falling tide to estimate that
the buoy drifted toward Pine Island.

Suggested steps:

1. Locate the tide marker from the photograph on the map.
2. Read a simple tide table.
3. Decide whether the water was rising or falling.
4. Compare three possible drift routes.
5. Rule out routes that conflict with time and current direction.
6. Select Pine Island as the next search area.

Language focus:

- `The water was rising / falling.`
- `If the current moved west...`
- `It could not be here because...`
- comparison and light conditional reasoning

Reward: `Tide Route Map`

Next chapter: `season-02-chapter-05`

### Chapter 05 — The Island Radio

On Pine Island, an unattended radio station is repeating the missing buoy's
signal. The station is not the source; it is a relay. Three antennas face
different coastal areas. The group rotates them and compares signal strength to
identify a small northern bay.

Suggested steps:

1. Discover the repeating radio signal.
2. Understand the difference between a source and a relay.
3. Follow instructions to adjust each antenna.
4. Compare weak, strong, and clear signals.
5. Complete the adjustment sequence in the correct order.
6. Use the strongest bearing to identify the northern bay.

Language focus:

- `Turn it slightly to the...`
- `The signal becomes stronger when...`
- `first / next / then / finally`
- `stronger / weaker / clearer`

Reward: `Radio Bearing`

Next chapter: `season-02-chapter-06`

### Chapter 06 — The Cave at Low Tide

The route to the bay passes a sea cave that is accessible only at low tide.
The group makes a safe plan before entering. Inside, they find Blue 7's broken
anchor clip and part of its protective casing, but not the buoy itself. Marks
and fresh driftwood show that the next tide pulled it back out.

Suggested steps:

1. Read the tide time and safety notice.
2. Choose appropriate equipment.
3. reject unsafe plans.
4. Enter with an adult field researcher.
5. inspect the broken anchor clip and drift marks.
6. infer that Blue 7 left the cave with the returning tide.

Language focus:

- `We should / should not...`
- `We must leave before...`
- `It is safer to...`
- cause and effect

Reward: `Broken Anchor Clip`

Next chapter: `season-02-chapter-07`

### Chapter 07 — Signal in the Storm

Rain and wind arrive early. From a safe viewpoint, Maple spots Blue 7 caught
near an old pier with its cable wrapped around driftwood. The children
coordinate with Dr. Chen and a trained rescue crew. Maple handles radio
communication while the crew recovers the buoy.

Suggested steps:

1. Locate Blue 7 from the viewpoint.
2. Assign safe roles to each team member.
3. relay the buoy's exact position by radio.
4. replace vague directions with precise instructions.
5. respond to a change in wind direction.
6. guide the crew to recover Blue 7 safely.

Language focus:

- clear directions and location phrases
- `If the wind changes, then...`
- `Move closer to / stay away from...`
- clarification with `Do you mean...?`

Reward: `Recovered Blue 7`

Next chapter: `season-02-chapter-08`

### Chapter 08 — The Song Beneath the Harbour

Back at the station, the group replaces Blue 7's battery, reconnects its cable,
and downloads the final recordings. The mystery signal is explained as three
overlapping sounds: the buoy striking driftwood, the island radio relay, and
the call of an orca group passing through the harbour.

Maple retells the investigation and connects every conclusion to evidence.
When Blue 7 begins recording again, the station receives a clear new sequence
of orca calls.

Suggested steps:

1. Restore Blue 7 with the research team.
2. Separate the three layers in the final recording.
3. Match each evidence item to the chapter where it was found.
4. order the full journey.
5. choose conclusions supported by evidence.
6. complete a short retelling of the investigation.
7. hear the restored harbour recording and receive the season badge.

Language focus:

- past-tense retelling
- `We first discovered...`
- `The most important clue was...`
- `This proved that...`
- summarising cause and effect

Reward: `Harbour Listener Badge`

Next chapter: none

Epilogue hook: after the main mystery is fully resolved, Blue 7 detects a faint
unidentified signal from the mountains. This may lead into Season 03, but it
must not make Season 02 feel incomplete.

## Map rules

1. The map is a background only.
2. Do not bake node numbers, route lines, locks, stars, text, progress, or Maple
   into the map artwork.
3. Render interactive nodes and routes as React overlays.
4. Store node coordinates as percentages, not pixels.
5. Preserve the full map aspect ratio. Do not crop it with `object-fit: cover`.
6. Suggested locations are harbour, research station, market, library, Pine
   Island radio station, tide cave, old pier, and research station return.
7. Final node coordinates must be chosen only after the Season 02 map exists.

## Audio rules

1. Audio is a story asset, not background decoration.
2. Every essential clip must have:
   - a replay button;
   - a visible transcript or accessible equivalent;
   - a progress or duration indicator;
   - a non-audio path to the same required conclusion.
3. Never autoplay chapter audio on page load.
4. Do not use YouTube or externally hosted clips.
5. Keep clips short, normally 3–15 seconds.
6. Preload only the active chapter's required audio.
7. Audio tasks may ask players to distinguish, order, or compare sounds. They
   must not claim to grade pronunciation.
8. Wildlife sounds must not be presented as English speech.

## Content implementation rules

- Each chapter should contain 5–7 meaningful steps.
- Mix dialogue, observation, listening or reading, inference, one concise
  language task, and an evidence reveal.
- Do not copy Practice game questions.
- Avoid one-word preschool recognition tasks.
- Do not require outside scientific knowledge; provide all necessary evidence.
- Use plain, natural English appropriate for A2+ to light B1.
- Wrong answers should receive a helpful hint tied to evidence, not only
  `Try again`.
- Keep dialogue short enough for mobile screens.
- Do not invent readable signs or labels that are absent from the final scene
  artwork.
- The visual, text, and audio versions of every clue must remain consistent.

## Visual consistency

- Use the same premium stylized 3D animated world as Season 01.
- Maple remains a girl with her established bow, eyelashes, explorer outfit,
  backpack, and visible beaver tail.
- Maple must remain recognisably a beaver, not become human-shaped.
- Human characters should look around 10–12 years old, not preschoolers or
  adults.
- Chapter scenes use 4:3 landscape artwork.
- Scenes must not contain question text, answer buttons, chapter numbers, UI,
  logos, or watermarks.
- Keep the tone adventurous and cinematic without showing children in
  uncontrolled danger.
- Season 02 may lean toward marine blue, teal, storm grey, amber light, and
  autumn red while remaining part of the SpeakUp Kids visual system.

## Accessibility and performance

- Provide meaningful alt text for every chapter scene.
- Decorative map and character layers use empty alt text.
- Chapter nodes must be native buttons with visible keyboard focus.
- Do not rely on colour alone for signal strength, chapter state, or answers.
- Transcripts must be keyboard accessible and readable by screen readers.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; move Maple immediately when reduced motion
  is enabled.
- Lazy-load inactive chapter scenes and audio.
- Do not store images or audio as base64 in source code.
