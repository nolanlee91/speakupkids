## Suggested lesson flow

1. Observe the real situation and identify the shared problem.
2. Listen to different users explain their needs or constraints.
3. Compare two or more proposed solutions.
4. Predict likely benefits and drawbacks.
5. Interpret the result of a field test or implementation step.
6. Revise or combine solutions using structured conversation turns.
7. Select and justify a final recommendation.
8. Complete a machine-graded Mini Check.

## Visual distinction

Collection 03 images show students actively surveying, testing or implementing solutions. Do not replace them with seated round-table discussions or presentation-card compositions used in earlier collections.

The artwork provides context only. Store prompts, dialogue, evidence and answers in structured lesson data.

Unit 04 content guardrail: treat the wheelchair user as an equal problem solver with agency. Compare the temporary ramp, doorway width, turning space, automatic-door control, visual alert, tactile paving, water-fountain heights and blocked path. Avoid framing accessibility as charity or assuming one solution works for every user.
